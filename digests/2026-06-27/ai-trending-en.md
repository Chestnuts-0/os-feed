## 3. Trend Signal Analysis

The dominant signal today is the **maturation of the "Agent Stack."** We are seeing a clear departure from monolithic "chatbots" to modular, composable agent systems. The explosive growth of **"Agent Harnesses"** (tools like `OpenClaw`, `Hermes Agent`, and `Claude Code` skills) indicates that developers are treating LLMs as workers rather than assistants. These harnesses provide the necessary scaffolding—memory, tool use, and skill management—to make agents reliable in production.

Simultaneously, the **standardization of connectivity** via MCP (Model Context Protocol) is accelerating. With official releases from AWS and Atlassian, the industry is moving towards a "plug-and-play" ecosystem for agents, reducing the friction of building custom integrations.

Another significant trend is the **democratization of complex media production**. Projects like `OpenMontage` and `waooAI/waoowaoo` suggest that AI agents are now capable of orchestrating multi-step creative pipelines (video, audio, graphics) that previously required human coordination. This points to a future where "creative coding" becomes an agentic discipline.

Finally, **local-first and privacy-centric AI** is gaining momentum. The high star counts for self-hosted tools (`AnythingLLM`, `Jan`, `OpenWebUI`) and local inference engines (`Ollama`, `Unsloth`) reflect a growing developer desire to own their data and reduce dependency on expensive, opaque cloud APIs.
